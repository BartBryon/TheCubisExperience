A LITTLE BIT ABOUT ME

Hey everyone,

my name is Bart . I'm a last year Information Management & Security student at the Thomas More College in Mechelen. I've been specializing in Cyber Security for the past 2,5 years. It's a fascinating subject and indispensable in today's business world.

During my studies I came in contact with the other specialization in our course, Data Management. Data has become a more valuable asset than oil and this intrigued me for two reasons:

-   It meant that this seemingly useless data can be gathered and analyzed and turned into a marketable asset.
-   It's one of the world's largest markets and I just wanted to know what it was all about.

I started messing around with it. Trying out software like Tableau and Power Bi. It turned out that I really enjoyed the process of working with these tools and data in general. That's Why I chose to follow an internship at Cubis Solutions, A Business Intelligence Company that consults clients on what to do with their huge amounts of gathered data.

The topic of my internship is Data Virtualization. For the companies that have tonnes of data, but lack the time/ability to extract, transform, or load their data, data virtualization might be the ideal option. So for the next 3 months I will be comparing SAP's HANA and Power BI's ability for Direct Queries.

I'm looking forward to it and I'm ready to learn!

MY EXPERIENCES AT CUBIS

Countdown

07 Febuary 2020

Just 3 more days until my Internship starts! And I have to say I'm getting pretty excited !

On Monday I'll be heading to the Malt in Boortmeerbeek, a business center that resides in a renovated malting plant. I've lived in the area for the last 14 years or so and I've noticed the building on several occasions. I've always thought it must be such a cool place to work! I can't believe I actually get to now.

The Deep Dive

16 Febuary 2020

So, the first week has passed here at Cubis, and I must say, it's everything I expected and more!

On Monday I met with my internship mentor Geert. He's a business manager at Cubis and has more than 6 years of experience consulting on business intelligence projects. He told me what my internship project would be, and it's a little different from what I initially thought it would be based on the internship ad I read when I first applied. Nevertheless it sounded really cool.

Lytix is Cubis's little sister. It's a company that focuses on business intelligence and data analytics on Microsoft's platform. Recently, on of their top consultants, wrote a ETL framework, called XTL (Lytix spelled backwards without the vowels), to standardize setting up a normalized Kimbal-oriented environment. The framework would semi-automize the consultants' tasks while on the job with a client.

Naturally, Cubis caught wind of this framework and wanted to build one themselves. This is where I come in the picture. It is my task to replicate the functionality of the XTL framework in SAP HANA. SAP is an environment I've never worked in before, what else, HANA is an in memory database. So I can't exactly copy paste the procedures from the XTL framework, but that would've made for a very boring project.

Wendnesday, Lytix organized a knowledge share evening. We ordered McDonald's and Sander (Creator of the XTL Framework), explained how to integrate the platform on the customers' network.

I started this week by studying both SAP HANA and the XTL Framework and started to build an idea of how I was going to "translate" it. I can't wait to actually start building it!

A Roller Coaster Ride

21 Febuary 2020

Week 2! boy oh boy, it had its ups and downs. But not necessarily in a bad way...

I started setting up the environment in which I am going to build the framework. SAP has a data provisioning agent. Without getting into details, It is a whole bunch of adapters that run on a separate machine through which you can connect all sorts of non-sap databases. Before you can use these adapters they need to be registered on your HANA database server... and that was all she wrote... I spent half a week troubleshooting the agent. My persistance paid off though. Last night I finally managed to establish a connection between my HANA database and Lytix's SQL Server Database!! I was ecstatic!

Cubis held an evening session this week. It is similar to Lytix's Knowledge shares. The office ordered catering and after a delicious pasta buffet, we gathered in the foyer to watch a presentation put together by some of the Cubis colleagues.

The subject of this month's evening session was the new SAP business objects 4.3. The presentation was very well put together. There were comparisons with the current version, and some live demo's. It gave me a better understanding of the scope of what products SAP actually has. Afterwards, I just hung around and had a couple of drinks with the colleagues. I must say, I'm loving the atmosphere here. It's very informal and we laugh a lot. (even without sacrificing productivity!)

Now That I fixed my connection, I can start building my model. Time to dive back into HANA and dimensional data modeling!

And We're Off

28 Febuary 2020

What's that saying? Time flies when you're having fun?

It's already the bottom of the 3rd inning, and we're starting to take off.

Monday started off pretty grim... I booted up the virtual machine and found out that the connection broke again. Bert, who is using an adapter on the same machine, was facing the same problem. Luckily, after some tweaking (and a server restart), the connection was up again. Bert's connection managed to stay stable, but mine was a bit flimsy. I'm still working on making it stable, but in the mean time, I drew up an architecture of what I wanted to accomplish with my project.

Essentially, I want to make the whole ETL process easier to implement for the consultants by automating repeatable tasks . It would standardize the way Cubis consultants work, making it easier for new consultants to learn the process and for colleagues to take over another consultant's work.

We're back on track and have our goal in sight! Eyes on the prize...
